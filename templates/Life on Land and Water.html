LIFE ON LAND & LIFE ON WATER

Exploring the Diversity of Terrestrial and Aquatic Species

Philippine Duck

Anas luzonica
Total Sighting (est): 15,000-30,000
Status: Stable/Least Concern

Sporting brown plumage and a distinctive blue-grey bill, this species charms with its subtle beauty and surprises with its adaptability.

White-breasted Waterhen

Amaurornis phoenicurus
Total Sighting (est): 10,000-100,000
Status: Stable/Least Concern

Adult White-breasted waterhens have mainly dark grey upperparts and flanks, and a white face, neck, and breast. The lower belly and undertail are cinnamon or white colored.

Garden Sunbird

Cinnyris jugularis
Total Sighting (est): 15,000-25,000
Status: Stable/Least Concern

A beautifully colored passerine bird from Southeast Asia. It has a long downward-curved bill which it uses to take nectar and capture insects.

Greater Musky Fruit Bat

Ptenochirus jagori
Total Sighting (est): Not specified yet
Status: Stable/Least Concern

A captivating creature of the night, found gracefully navigating the tropical forests of Southeast Asia. These fascinating bats play a vital role in their ecosystems.

Luzon Giant Cloud Rat

Phloeomys pallidus
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Stable/Concerned

This rodent has a relatively long pelage, which also covers the tail. The color is highly variable, but it is usually pale brown-grey or white with some dark brown or black patches.

Lion

Panthera leo
Total Sighting (est): 23,000-39,000
Status: Decreasing/Vulnerable

The lion is a big wild cat with short, tawny-colored fur and white underparts. The long tail ends with a black tuft.

Tokay Gecko

Gekko gecko
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Stable/Least Concern

The skin of Tokay geckos is soft to the touch and is generally gray with red speckles. However, Tokay geckos can change the color of their skin to blend into the environment.

Reticulated Python

Malayopython reticulatus
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Stable/Least Concern

Is a non-venomous snake native to South and Southeast Asia. It is the world's longest snake and is among the three heaviest snakes.

Common House Gecko

Hemidactylus frenatus
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Stable/Least Concern

A small lizard native to Southeast Asia. They are named so because they are often seen climbing walls of houses and other buildings in search of insects.

Philippine Warty Pig

Sus philippensis
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Vulnerable

A wild pig species with a distinctive "mane" and warty face. Important for nutrient cycling in forests.

Philippine Long-Tailed Macaque

Macaca fascicularis philippensis
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Least Concern

Lives in group and eat fruits, leaves, and small animals, making it very adaptable. However, its population is decreasing due to habitat loss and trapping.

Collared Kingfisher

Todiramphus chloris
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Least Concern

It hunts insects, crabs and small fish by perching and quickly diving its prey. This species is widespread and easily spotted due to its loud calls.

Olive Ridley Turtle

Lepidochelys olivacea
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Vulnerable/Endangered

A small to medium sea turtle known for its olive colored shell. They are famous for nesting events called "arribadas". They feed on jellyfish, small fish, crustaceans.

Green Turtle

Chelonia mydas
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Endangered

Large herbivorous sea turtle often found near coral reefs and seagrass beds. Adults eat sea grass, helping maintain healthy marine ecosystems.

Hawksbill Turtle

Eretmochelys imbricata
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Critically Endangered

Critically endangered turtle known for its beautifully patterned shell. They help control sponge populations on coral reefs.

Whale Shark

Rhincodon typus
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Endangered

The world's largest fish. A gentle filter feeder eating plankton and small fish. Important to ocean plankton balance.

Blacktip Reef Shark

Carcharhinus melanopterus
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Near Threatened

A small reef shark with black-tipped fins. Lives in shallow water and coral reefs. Plays a role in keeping fish populations balanced.

Manta Rays

Mobula alfredi, Mobula birostris
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Vulnerable/Endangered

Large, gentle rays called "sea butterflies". Filter feeding on plankton. Highly intelligent and long lived.

Spinner Dolphin

Stenella longirostris
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Vulnerable

Small, active dolphins known for spinning jumps. Feed on small fish and squid. Important part of marine food chain.

Dwarf Sperm Whale

Kogia sima
Total Sighting (est): Unknown
Status: Data Deficient

A small, shy whale species rarely seen alive. Known for releasing a cloud of ink-like fluid to escape predators.
