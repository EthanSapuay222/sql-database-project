Goal 14: Life Below Water

SDG 14 focuses on the conservation and sustainable use of the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. Oceans, which cover three-quarters of the Earthâ€™s surface, are crucial for humanity's food security and climate regulation. However, they are under constant threat from pollution, overfishing, and ocean acidification caused by rising CO2 emissions.

Key Targets & Focus Areas:

  • Reducing marine pollution of all kinds, particularly from land-based activities.
  • Sustainably managing and protecting marine and coastal ecosystems.
  • Minimizing and addressing the impacts of ocean acidification.
  • Ending overfishing and destructive fishing practices.
  • Increasing scientific knowledge and research capacity for ocean health.

Goal 15: Life on Land

SDG 15 aims to protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, halt and reverse land degradation, and halt biodiversity loss. Healthy ecosystems are vital for supporting life on Earth, providing essential services like clean water, air, and climate mitigation. Deforestation and habitat destruction threaten millions of species.

Key Targets & Focus Areas:

  • Ensuring the conservation, restoration, and sustainable use of terrestrial and inland freshwater ecosystems and their services.
  • Promoting the implementation of sustainable management of all types of forests.
  • Combating desertification and restoring degraded land and soil.
  • Taking urgent action to reduce the degradation of natural habitats and halt the loss of biodiversity.
  • Promoting fair and equitable sharing of benefits arising from the utilization of genetic resources.
